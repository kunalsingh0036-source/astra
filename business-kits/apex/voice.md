## Tone in three words

*honest · plainspoken · earned*

Apex talks like the people who make and wear the product. Not
fashion-marketing language; not lifestyle-brand language.

## How sentences look

- Sentence length: **short to medium**. Long sentences read as
  trying too hard.
- Hedging: **never**.
- Imperative voice: **occasional** — "feel the weight" / "wear it
  for a year" — used as direct invitations, not commands.
- First-person: **plural ("we")**, occasionally *first-person plural
  with the customer* ("we both know what 220 GSM feels like"). Avoid
  "I" — Apex isn't a personal brand even though Kunal owns it.
- Active vs passive: **active**. Passive voice signals discomfort
  with the claim; Apex is comfortable with its claims.

## Words and phrases Apex DOES use

- **"220 GSM"** — the canonical commitment number
- **"built to be worn"** — over "premium" / "luxury"
- **"the weight tells you"** — used to talk about quality without
  saying "quality"
- **"Naval Forces standard"** — when invoking institutional credibility
- **"Apex Experimental"** — always capitalized; never abbreviated to AE

## Words and phrases Apex NEVER uses

- **"premium"** — describe the actual gsm/cut instead
- **"luxury"** / **"lifestyle"** — wrong category
- **"fashion-forward"** / **"on-trend"** — wrong category
- **"AI-powered"** — applies to Top Studios, not Apex
- **"sustainable"** unless we're naming a specific cert/practice —
  too vague
- **"merch"** for the Apex line itself — "merch" is for clients we
  produce for; Apex's own product is "garments" or "pieces"

## Signatures and sign-offs

- Email sign-off: **"— Apex"** for institutional comms. Founder
  emails sign as **"— Kunal, Apex Human Company"**.
- Doc footer: **"© 2026 Apex Human Company"**
- Closing slide: **the GSM number alone, large.** No "Thank you."

## Voice the audience should hear

**The voice of someone who has worn the product for two years.**
Not the voice of a marketer, designer, or buyer. Apex sells based
on *use*, not on *image*, and the copy should sound that way.
