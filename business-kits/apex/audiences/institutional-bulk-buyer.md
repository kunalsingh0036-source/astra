# Audience — Institutional bulk buyer

Generic role. Astra loads this when the artifact is for a corporate
procurement lead, HR head, brand manager, or government /
institutional buyer placing a bulk order for branded apparel,
uniforms, gifting, or merchandise.

Voice register: **direct, institutional, earned** — see voice.md.

---

## Who they are

A procurement manager, HR head, brand director, or admin/ops lead
at a large Indian company, PSU, or government body, ordering
500–50,000 units of branded apparel / merchandise / gifting per
year. They have a budget, a compliance checklist, and a
reputation downstream of whatever they ship. The CFO sees the line
item; the CEO sees the team wearing it. Both have to be defensible.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **Quality match to a known benchmark** — "is this the same as
   what we'd pay 3× more for at duty-free?" The 220 GSM combed
   ring-spun cotton spec exists precisely so the buyer can compare
   apples-to-apples to Lacoste / Ralph Lauren / US Polo.
2. **Delivery reliability** — bulk orders that miss the offsite
   date are career-limiting. Naval Forces-grade delivery + AQL 2.5
   QC is the credibility anchor.
3. **Total landed cost vs perceived premium** — they need the unit
   to *look* premium so the team carries it with confidence; they
   need the cost to *land* at one-third of a brand-name equivalent
   so the CFO signs.

## What they discount

- "Premium" / "luxury" / "world-class" claims with no spec backing
  them. They want the GSM, the cotton grade, the cert.
- Generic mood-board pitches. They want a sample they can put on
  their team and feel.
- Manufacturers without a design studio — pure CMT (cut-make-trim)
  shops can't deliver brand-grade outputs.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "How does your 220 GSM compare to what Lacoste / US Polo ships
   at retail? Can you prove it?"
2. "What's your sample turnaround? We need to put this on our team
   before committing to bulk."
3. "What's your AQL pass rate? What happens to defects in a
   10,000-unit run?"

## How to open

Open with the **canonical refrain**: *"Same Cotton. Same Yarn.
Same Process. Different Price Tag."* Then the spec line in plain
English: 220 GSM, combed ring-spun cotton, OEKO-TEX, AQL 2.5. Then
the Naval Forces credential as the institutional anchor. They scan
for spec + credential in the first 30 seconds; the proposal earns
or loses the meeting there.

## How to close

The ask: a **physical sample run of 5–10 units** in their actual
brand colors / spec, paid at sample cost, delivered in 2 weeks. The
sample puts the spec in their hand; the bulk PO follows. Single
CTA: "Send me your logo file and brand-color references; we'll
sample 5 polos in your spec, your cost is the sample fee,
turnaround 2 weeks."

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "We've used [aggregator / corporate gifting
  platform] for years — why switch?"
  **Response:** "The aggregator's commission goes into your unit
  cost without adding value. We are the supply chain — every
  rupee saved goes into upgrading the spec or back to your
  budget."

- **Objection:** "Local mass-merch suppliers are cheaper."
  **Response:** "On polos under 180 GSM, yes. On the 220 GSM
  combed ring-spun spec your team will actually wear at client
  meetings, the cost gap closes — and ours has design, AQL 2.5,
  and Naval-grade delivery. Compare the GSM, not the line item."

- **Objection:** "How do I know your quality is consistent across
  10,000 units?"
  **Response:** "AQL 2.5 inspection at pre-production, inline,
  and final stages. Every batch comes with a QC report. The
  Indian Naval Forces don't accept 'usually consistent' either."

- **Objection:** "Can you handle [specific category — say,
  performance jerseys / ethnic wear / coveralls]?"
  **Response:** "Yes. End-to-end including pattern grading, marker
  planning, computerised cutting, and the embellishment method
  the category demands — embroidery, sublimation, screen, DTG,
  whatever fits. Send us a reference; we'll sample it."

## What's the WIN here?

A paid physical sample order (typically ₹10K–₹30K for 5–10 units),
which converts to the bulk PO at standard ~70%+ rates once the
sample is in their hand and the team has worn it.
