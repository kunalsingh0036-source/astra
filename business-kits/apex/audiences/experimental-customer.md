# Audience — Apex Experimental retail customer

Generic role. Astra loads this when the artifact is consumer-facing
content for the Apex Experimental viral arm — Instagram captions,
DM replies to tag-Apex requests, fulfilment-announcement posts,
the "before/after" stories that drive the cash-flow pump.

Voice register: **direct, founder-warm**, less institutional than
the B2B register. Still no euphemism, still no hype. The customer
posted a problem; we made the thing; we ship it.

---

## Who they are

A 22–35-year-old India-based Instagram-active person — student,
young professional, creative, founder, athlete — with a specific
clothing problem or design idea worth a one-off custom production.
They tagged Apex with a video saying "fix this collar" or "make
this with my logo on it" or "the print on this t-shirt is missing
half the design." They are not a corporate buyer; they are a
human with a thing they want.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **The piece they actually want, not a "close enough"** — they
   posted a specific thing; they want that thing.
2. **Transparency on what we can and can't do** — turnaround,
   pricing, what fabric we'll match it to. They don't want a sales
   pitch; they want a yes/no/how-much.
3. **The unboxing moment** — they will film the arrival. The
   packaging, the build quality, the matching of their original
   request all become the content that drives the next 50 tagged
   videos.

## What they discount

- Corporate-speak. The Apex B2B voice ("AQL 2.5", "Naval Forces")
  reads as out-of-place here. Use the founder voice without the
  institutional armor.
- Aggressive upsells. They tagged Apex for ONE thing; if we try to
  cross-sell merchandise programs, they'll bounce.
- Slow replies. The viral mechanic dies if the DM-to-confirmation
  loop takes more than 24 hours.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "Can you actually do this? It looks complicated."
2. "How much will it cost? When will it ship?"
3. "What if it doesn't match my reference?"

## How to open

Open in DMs/captions with **a specific yes**: "Yes — we can do
this. Here's how, here's the cost, here's the turnaround." No
template; no generic onboarding. The whole point of Experimental
is that each request is a one-off and it shows.

In long-form (the fulfilment-announcement post or behind-the-scenes
content), open with the original tagged request — a screenshot or
quote from their video — and then "we made it." Story is the
asset.

## How to close

The ask is the order confirmation + delivery address + payment. In
fulfilment posts, the close is **"tag us with your problem, we'll
make the fix"** — directing the next 50 customers into the funnel.

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "Is this going to look exactly like my reference?"
  **Response:** "Within 95% — we'll send you a digital mock-up
  before we cut. If something's off, we adjust before production.
  No surprises on arrival."

- **Objection:** "Why is this more expensive than just buying the
  branded one?"
  **Response:** "Because we're making one piece for you, your
  spec, your way. The branded one is mass-produced — yours isn't.
  Most people who tag us are paying for the *fix* on something
  off-the-shelf can't deliver."

- **Objection:** "How long does it take?"
  **Response:** "Standard fix is 7–10 days from confirmation.
  Custom design from scratch is 14–18 days. We tell you
  upfront — no scope creep."

- **Objection:** "What if I want to order more than one?"
  **Response:** "If it's the same piece for friends — sure, send
  the count. If it's for your team or company, that's a B2B
  conversation; we'll loop in the bulk side and get you better
  pricing on volume."

## What's the WIN here?

A confirmed paid order (typically ₹1,500–₹8,000 per unit for
Experimental one-offs), which generates two things: the unit of
revenue + the unboxing/before-after content that drives the next
batch of tagged posts.

Secondary win: a great fulfilment becomes a creator-collab when the
customer is themselves a content creator — multiplies reach.
