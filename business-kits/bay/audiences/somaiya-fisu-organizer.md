# Audience — Somaiya FISU squash organizer

Specific named target. Astra loads this when the artifact is for the
Somaiya University / FISU (Fédération Internationale du Sport
Universitaire — World University Sports) organising contact for the
squash discipline. The relationship is alumni / institutional —
Kunal coached the 2024 World University Championship squad and is
both a credential and an asset for the next cycle.

Voice register: **institutional ("Uncompromising")** with founder
credential foregrounded. India Rank 18 + 2024 WUC Bronze is
non-negotiable here — it's the entire reason this conversation
exists.

---

## Who they are

A senior administrator, athletic-program director, or international-
sports coordinator at Somaiya University (Mumbai) or a counterpart
at the All India University Sports Council, coordinating with FISU
for the World University Championship and World University Games
squash discipline. They program-manage the Indian university
squash effort across selection, training camps, travel, and
international competition. They know Kunal as the 2024 WUC
head coach.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **A delivery partner for the next FISU cycle** — training camps,
   athlete pipeline, venue, coaching. The 2024 bronze proves the
   delivery; they need a 2026/2028 partner that's institution-grade,
   not a one-off coaching engagement.
2. **Brand association with a performance institution** — Somaiya
   benefits when its university squash programme is seen alongside
   a Mumbai-based performance institution that runs glass courts,
   lab, S&C, recovery. BAY raises Somaiya's institutional profile;
   Somaiya's FISU pipeline raises BAY's.
3. **CSR / institutional funding fit** — FISU efforts often need
   non-tuition funding (travel, kit, training-camp residency). BAY's
   Outreach Program and equipment partnership tiers can plug into
   this — a structural collaboration, not a single-event request.

## What they discount

- Generic "we'd love to help" outreach without specifics.
- Treatment as a vendor rather than as an institutional partner.
- Pure-coaching pitches — they have selection panels and federation
  coaches; the value-add is institutional infrastructure, not just
  another coaching voice.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "What does a structural partnership look like, concretely? Camp
   weeks? Year-round access? Match-play opportunities?"
2. "What's the commercial structure? Are you charging Somaiya, or
   is this MoU territory?"
3. "How does this scale beyond squash if it works?"

## How to open

Open with the **2024 WUC reference** as the relationship anchor —
not as a credential being introduced, but as a shared history
already on the record. Then the institutional offer: BAY's facility
+ programs + Kunal's continued coaching role for the 2026/2028
cycle, structured as an MoU rather than a transactional engagement.

## How to close

The ask: a **memorandum of understanding** for the 2026–2028 FISU
cycle covering — training-camp residency at BAY (Badhwar Park
near-term, Excellence Centre when ready), athlete-pipeline
collaboration with Somaiya's selection process, Kunal's continued
coaching role, and a defined commercial structure (CSR-funded
preferred; cost-recovery acceptable).

Single CTA: "30 minutes to scope the 2026 cycle and draft the MoU."

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "We work with the federation directly — what's the
  added layer?"
  **Response:** "The federation handles selection and oversight;
  BAY handles delivery — facility, S&C, recovery, lab, year-round
  access. The 2024 bronze didn't happen because of selection alone;
  it happened because of the coaching, S&C, and competitive
  exposure layered on top. That's what BAY institutionalises for
  the next cycle."

- **Objection:** "What's the cost to Somaiya?"
  **Response:** "Preferred structure is CSR / institutional
  funding — there are 3 mandate-fulfilling partners (Tata, Reliance,
  JSW typical) for whom this fits Olympic-pathway CSR. Cost-recovery
  to Somaiya is acceptable as a backup; we'll model both."

- **Objection:** "Why BAY specifically? There are other Mumbai
  squash venues."
  **Response:** "Most Mumbai squash venues are clubs with court
  time. BAY is a performance institution — courts, S&C, recovery,
  lab, founder-coach with PSA + WUC credentials — under one roof.
  That's the FISU-grade infrastructure profile."

- **Objection:** "Can we pilot with one camp before MoU?"
  **Response:** "Yes. Camp pilot first, MoU when the operating
  rhythm is proven. We'll scope a 5-day residential pilot with the
  next selected squad."

## What's the WIN here?

A signed MoU for the 2026–2028 FISU cycle covering training
residency, athlete-pipeline collaboration, Kunal's coaching role,
and CSR-funded commercial structure — OR (consolation outcome) a
single training-camp pilot with the next selected squad as a
no-MoU first step.
