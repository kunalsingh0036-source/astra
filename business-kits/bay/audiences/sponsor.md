# Audience — Sponsor / equipment partnership lead

Generic role. Astra loads this when the artifact is for a
brand-marketing or sponsorship lead at an equipment, apparel,
nutrition, wellness, or category-adjacent brand evaluating a BAY
partnership across the three published tiers (Official / Category /
Referral).

Voice register: **institutional ("Uncompromising")**. See voice.md.

---

## Who they are

A brand manager, sponsorship lead, or marketing director at a
sports-equipment brand (Asics, Salming, Tecnifibre, Wilson),
performance-apparel brand (Decathlon, Adidas, Nike, Skechers
performance), nutrition / supplement brand (Optimum, MuscleBlaze,
GNC, Wellbeing Nutrition), or category-adjacent wellness brand
(cryotherapy, breathwork, hydration). They run multi-property
sponsorship programs and need to defend each line item to the
brand-marketing director. Decisions take 6–10 weeks typically;
contract terms matter as much as the surface offer.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **Reach + content output** — how many eyeballs, how often,
   what quality? BAY's 200+ athletes + 50+ sessions/month + 50+
   weekly content pieces is the volume claim; they verify before
   signing.
2. **Category alignment + exclusivity terms** — they don't want
   to share screen time with a competing brand. The Tier 1
   (Official Partner) "category-exclusive" line is the lever.
3. **Prestige association** — Olympic ambition, India Rank 18
   founder, 2024 World University Bronze, "performance institution
   not academy" — these signal that BAY is the kind of property
   the brand wants to be photographed next to.

## What they discount

- Vanity metrics. Instagram followers without engagement is noise.
- "We'll be huge soon" projections. They underwrite current state
  with reasonable trajectory, not aspiration.
- Founder personality without competitive credentials. Kunal's
  India Rank 18 + 2024 WUC Bronze + Naval Officer is the
  authority anchor; lead with credentials, not personality.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "What's the actual content volume + engagement? Show me the
   last 3 months of post analytics."
2. "What does category-exclusivity actually cover? Sub-categories?
   Competitor products our co-brands carry?"
3. "What's the activation calendar? Posts, stories, events,
   athlete appearances — what do we get when?"

## How to open

Open with the **founder credential** + the **performance-institution
frame** in one sentence each. Sponsors fund prestige + reach; both
require BAY to read as institutional, not as a coaching service.
Then the current-state numbers (200+ athletes, 50+ sessions/mo, 50+
weekly content pieces, 1+ locations) — concrete, dated, verifiable.

## How to close

The ask: **Tier-1 Official Partnership at category exclusivity, 12-
month term, [contract value TBD per brand]** with a defined
content + activation calendar. Mid-tier alternative: Tier 2
Category Partner at 6-month minimum if they aren't ready to commit
to category exclusivity yet.

Close with a single CTA: "30 minutes to walk through the activation
calendar and the category-exclusivity scope. Diary attached."

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "Squash is a niche sport — what's the reach?"
  **Response:** "Squash is the second-fastest-growing racquet
  sport globally; India is targeting Olympic medals. The audience
  isn't mass — it's CXOs, sports families, and the Tier-1 city
  professional class. That's the audience your brand actually
  wants for this category, not mass."

- **Objection:** "We'd want to sponsor when you have 1,000 athletes,
  not 200."
  **Response:** "By 1,000 athletes, the category will already
  be locked. Olympic 2032 / 2036 is the targeted horizon; the
  brands that show up in 2026 own the institutional association.
  Right now is the founder pricing window."

- **Objection:** "What's category-exclusive cover?"
  **Response:** "Category-exclusive means: across BAY's 50+
  weekly content pieces and 200+ athletes, no other brand in
  your category appears in branded content, on athlete kit, or
  at BAY-hosted events. Adjacent categories are open. Specific
  sub-category boundaries we'll define together in the term sheet."

- **Objection:** "We need to see ROI on the sponsorship."
  **Response:** "ROI on a 12-month deal: athlete-content output
  contracted as deliverables, on-court branded visibility at all
  sessions, founder-channel mentions, and category-exclusive lock-
  out from competitors during the term. We measure delivery
  against the activation calendar — you measure brand lift on
  your end."

## What's the WIN here?

A signed Tier 1 (Official Partner) contract — 12-month,
category-exclusive — with a defined content + activation calendar
and an agreed contract value. Tier 2 (Category Partner) at 6-month
minimum is the consolation outcome; both fund the operating
runway.
