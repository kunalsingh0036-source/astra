# Audience — Excellence-track athlete recruit

Generic role. Astra loads this when the artifact is recruiting an
elite or sub-elite squash athlete (national-circuit junior, PSA
hopeful, top-100 India ranking, or scholarship candidate) into the
Excellence track of BAY.

Voice register: **founder voice ("Uncompromising")**. Direct
second-person ("you handle the game"); founder credential
mentioned because India Rank 18 + 2024 WUC Bronze is the only
authority that lands with this audience.

---

## Who they are

A 14–22-year-old India-based squash player with junior-national or
PSA-circuit ambitions. They've already trained somewhere — academy,
private coach, club program — and have plateaued or hit a ceiling.
Their parents underwrite the program (financially and
logistically); they make the call on whether to take it. They scan
forums and Instagram for who's actually winning, not who's
marketing.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **Competitive infrastructure they can't get elsewhere** — glass
   courts, S&C, recovery, lab, coaching by someone who has *been*
   there. The 5-story Excellence Centre architecture is the
   long-term answer; the current Badhwar Park program + Kunal's
   personal coaching is the bridge.
2. **A coach who has the ranking they want** — not a former player
   who quit, not an academy lead who never broke top-50. India
   Rank 18 + 2024 WUC Bronze + PSA Tour + "led the national squad
   to a historic bronze" is the founder-credential proof.
3. **The pathway after** — Olympic squad consideration, Ivy League
   scholarship, PSA Tour visibility. Athletes don't commit to a
   program for the program; they commit for the next door.

## What they discount

- "Best academy in Mumbai" claims without tournament results
- "Holistic" coaching language without specifics — they want the
  S&C protocol, the recovery routine, the periodisation.
- Marketing language. Athletes scan for credentials and program
  specifics; they bounce on hype.
- Pricing-led messaging. Cost matters but isn't first; outcome is.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "What does the weekly schedule actually look like? How many
   hours of court time, how many of S&C, what's the recovery
   block?"
2. "What's the periodisation across the year? How does it sync to
   Junior Nationals / National Circuit dates?"
3. "What kind of athletes have come through this program? What
   results?"

## How to open

Open with **Kunal's competitive credential** in one sentence —
India Rank 18, 2024 WUC Bronze, "led the national squad" — and
then the Excellence-track promise: court time, S&C, recovery, lab,
coaching, with a periodisation plan synced to the athlete's
competitive calendar.

Skip the "performance institution" framing — that's for sponsors.
For athletes, the language is: *"Twelve months. Four hundred
sessions. One Olympic squad. This is where the 1% is forged."*

## How to close

The ask: a **trial week** at Badhwar Park with full program
exposure (sessions, S&C, recovery, founder-coached match analysis),
followed by a 12-month commitment if both sides agree. Single CTA:
"Bring your kit and your competitive calendar. One trial week.
We'll show you the program; you decide."

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "How is this different from my current academy?"
  **Response:** "Most academies coach the technique. This program
  coaches the athlete — sport, S&C, recovery, mental, periodisation,
  and the institutional architecture around your competitive year.
  And it's coached by someone whose name you'd recognise on the
  India ranking — that matters when the room gets tight."

- **Objection:** "Mumbai is far / expensive / not where my family is."
  **Response:** "The trial week is to see if the program is worth
  the move. If it's not, you've lost a week. If it is, the
  scholarship pathway and the Ivy League outcomes are the
  long-term return — those don't happen at home academies."

- **Objection:** "What about scholarships? My parents care about
  that."
  **Response:** "Scholar-Athletes is a structural pillar of the
  program — Ivy League scholarships are part of the Outreach arm
  of the long-horizon build. We invest in academic + competitive
  development together; the scholarship math compounds the
  program's return."

- **Objection:** "I'm not Olympic-track. Am I in the wrong program?"
  **Response:** "The Excellence track is by application — the
  Community and Grassroots programs serve sub-elite and developing
  athletes who want competitive infrastructure without the Olympic-
  pathway intensity. Street to Elite is the bridge between them and
  Excellence. Trial the right program; we'll route you."

## What's the WIN here?

A confirmed trial week (calendar block + travel + program
exposure), which converts to a 12-month Excellence-track commitment
in roughly 60–80% of cases when the athlete is the right fit.
