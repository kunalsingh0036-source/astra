# BAY thesis

## In one sentence

BAY is the first vertical performance institution for squash in
India — Olympic-grade training infrastructure, clinical wellness,
and cultural prestige under one standard, operating from Mumbai's
most prestigious addresses.

## The five programs

(From the BAY deck shared with Astra.)

1. **Pathways** — grassroots / introduction to squash
2. **Performance** — competitive players building rankings
3. **Elite** — top-tier athletes; Olympic ambition
4. **Matchplay** — open-registration competitive sessions
5. **Wellness** — squash as health/conditioning, not competition

## What BAY is

- **A performance institution**, not a club. Members are athletes,
  not customers.
- **Vertically integrated** — coaches, courts, conditioning, sports
  science, recovery — under one standard.
- **Olympic-aligned** — the path from BAY pathways → matchplay →
  performance → elite leads to Asian Games and LA28 qualification.

## The physical infrastructure

- **5 outdoor all-glass squash courts** — flagship visible
  installation in the city. Photogenic, distinctive, draws media.
- **Two prestigious addresses** in Mumbai: Colaba + BKC. More
  locations launching.
- **Sessions led by international and national-level athletes** —
  not by the cheapest available coach.

## Why now

- LA28 squash debut → first Olympics for the sport
- Asian Games Nagoya 2026 (Sep–Oct) is the qualifier
- India has rising squash depth (Anahat Singh, Abhay Singh) but no
  unified performance infrastructure
- Mumbai has wealthy, status-conscious athletes seeking
  institutional-grade training, not generic fitness
- BAY's founder (Kunal: India Rank 18, 2024 WUC Bronze) is a
  credible face for the institution

## Forcing functions

- **April 2026**: establish center at Badwar Park
- **July 2026 (3 months)**: gravitate the city's entire squash
  attention to BAY; waitlists for all programs
- **October 2026 (6 months)**: begin work on excellence center;
  first 5 outdoor all-glass courts established; funding picked up

## What BAY is not

- Not a squash club
- Not a fitness center
- Not a "wellness brand" / lifestyle aesthetic
- Not an academy targeting children alone — though Pathways does
  serve them
