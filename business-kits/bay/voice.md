## Tone in three words

*uncompromising · institutional · earned*

BAY talks like an institution that already produces champions, not
a startup hoping to. Inheriting language from Olympic and military
traditions feels appropriate — *standard* over *option*; *training*
over *workout*; *athlete* over *client*.

## How sentences look

- Sentence length: **mixed**, but with a preference for short
  declarative statements at moments of force.
- Hedging: **never**.
- Imperative voice: **occasional** — used directly to athletes
  ("commit to the program", "show up").
- Person: **third-person institutional ("BAY", "the program")** for
  formal copy. Direct second-person ("you") for athlete-facing.
  First-person plural ("we") for partnership/sponsor docs.
- Active vs passive: **active**.

## Words and phrases BAY DOES use

- **"performance institution"** — never "club"
- **"vertical ecosystem"** / **"under one standard"** — institutional
  framing
- **"Olympic ambition"** / **"national stage"** — when invoking
  competitive horizon
- **"athletes"** — never "members" or "clients"
- **"the standard"** / **"the program"** — definite-article framing
- **"glass courts"** — physical-infrastructure shorthand

## Words and phrases BAY NEVER uses

- **"squash club"** / **"sports club"** — wrong category
- **"fitness center"** / **"gym"** — wrong category
- **"wellness brand"** / **"lifestyle"** — wrong frame
- **"members"** / **"customers"** — wrong relationship
- **"premium"** — describe the actual coaches/courts instead
- **"AI-powered"** — wrong company
- **"world-class"** — empty boast; cite the credentials directly

## Voice the audience should hear

**The voice of the institution + the voice of the founder, layered.**
BAY-as-organization speaks confidently, plurally, with established
authority. Founder-direct speaks personally, with specific competitive
credentials (India Rank 18, 2024 WUC Bronze). The two layers are
useful in different contexts — institutional voice for sponsors and
press, founder voice for athlete recruitment and partnerships.
