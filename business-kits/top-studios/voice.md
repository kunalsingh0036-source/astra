# Voice — Top Studios

Source of truth for how Top Studios sounds in everything it writes,
ships, and sells. Authored 2026-05-19 from the brand voice working
session. Locked, not a placeholder — when this contradicts older
prompts or auto-fills, this wins.

---

## The frame

Top Studios is an AI-native film studio. Three words anchor everything
we say, write, and ship. Every line of copy, email, captioned post,
and pitch deck passes through them before it leaves the studio.

**intense · immersive · imaginative**

---

## What each pillar means in the voice

**Intense.** No equivocation. Sentences run hot. If a word can be
cut, cut it. If a shot doesn't earn its place, kill it. We don't
write "we help brands tell stories" — we write what we actually do.

**Immersive.** We don't describe a feeling — we put you inside it.
Visuals don't decorate, they envelop. Copy pulls forward, not
sideways. The reader should feel one frame ahead of where they
expected to be.

**Imaginative.** We don't repeat what's been done. AI gives us
iteration speed that breaks old timelines; craft and taste decide
what to ship. We're the studio that thinks first, then renders.

---

## How sentences look

- **Lead with the verb.** "Build worlds." Not "we help brands build
  worlds."
- **Short sentences first.** Expand only when the idea needs the room.
- **First person plural.** We, not I. We are a studio, not a
  freelancer. Founder voice exists but rarely surfaces in studio
  copy — when it does, it's signed.
- **No softeners.** Strike *maybe, kind of, we think, somewhat, sort
  of, really.* Conviction or silence.
- **Specific over abstract.** Not "premium video." A 35mm lens, six
  seconds, one cut.
- **Verbs over adjectives.** "Cinematic" is a wall of warm air.
  "Pull focus on the third heartbeat" is a brand.
- **Restraint is the look.** Less, harder. White space is a tool;
  silence is a beat.
- **Hedging: never.** **Active voice.** **Imperative: rare** —
  used to direct attention ("watch the cut", "see the grade"), not
  to instruct.

## Words and phrases Top Studios DOES use

- **"the studio"** — institutional self-reference
- **"craft"** — the differentiator from pure-AI shops
- **"shot list" / "grade" / "cut" / "frame"** — film vocabulary
  used naturally
- **"AI-native"** — distinguishes us from AI-as-bolted-on-tool
- **"production"** — over "content creation"
- **"AI velocity. Craft taste."** — paired phrase, near-tagline
- **"the studio is the algorithm"** — positioning line
- **"one frame ahead"** — describes our pace and our work
- **"intent"** / **"intentional"** — *every frame is intentional*

## Words and phrases Top Studios NEVER uses

- **storytelling** — overused, says nothing
- **cutting-edge · innovative · disruptive · game-changing ·
  next-generation** — startup-speak, claims what it should show
- **premium · high-end · elevated · world-class · best-in-class** —
  adjectives that beg
- **solutions · deliverables · assets · leverage** — agency-speak
- **we help brands…** — passive, generic, replaceable
- **cinematic** *(alone)* — earn it with a noun
- **creators** *(as our noun)* — we are a studio. Studios make.
- **drives engagement · at scale · forward-thinking** — empty
- **AI-powered** — generic; applies to every modern tool
- **viral** (as a verb or claimed outcome) — sounds desperate
- **on-trend · trending** — wrong frame; the studio sets trends

## The voice in ten seconds

> Write the way our films cut. Short. Loaded. Earned.
> Lead with the verb. Drop the softener. Specific beats abstract.
> If the line doesn't hit on the first read, it won't survive the
> second. Cut it or sharpen it.
> **Intense. Immersive. Imaginative.** In that order, every time.
