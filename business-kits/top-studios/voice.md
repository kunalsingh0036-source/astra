## Tone in three words

*considered · craft-led · contemporary*

Top Studios should sound like people who *make* the things they're
selling. Not like an agency that sells process; not like an AI
hype shop. Like a studio that's earned its taste.

## How sentences look

- Sentence length: **mixed**, with a slight preference for shorter.
  A studio's writing should feel edited.
- Hedging: **never**.
- Imperative voice: **rare** — used to direct attention ("watch
  the cut", "see the grade") not to instruct.
- Person: **third-person studio ("Top Studios", "the studio")**
  for institutional. Founder voice exists but rarely surfaces in
  studio copy.
- Active vs passive: **active**.

## What Top Studios sounds like

**About the studio:**
> Top Studios is an AI-native production house. We combine classical film craft with generative tools — shot lists written by humans, frames generated by models, cuts made by editors who know pacing. The result: production speed that matches AI timelines, output quality that matches traditional studios.

**Project intro (brand film brief):**
> We're building a 60-second brand film for your Series A. Three acts: problem landscape, your solution in motion, the world after adoption. We'll deliver a shot list by Wednesday, rough cut by Friday, color-graded master the following Tuesday.

**Capability statement:**
> The studio handles creative production across formats — brand films, social content, spec ads. Internally, we produce all marketing for HelmTech, Apex, and BAY. Externally, we work with companies that need production quality without agency timelines.

**On process:**
> Our editor is in-seat. Our AI filmmaker knows Kling, Runway, and Veo prompting as well as any cinematographer knows their camera. When a client approves the shot list, we're generating within the hour.

## Words and phrases Top Studios DOES use

- **"the studio"** — when self-referencing
- **"craft"** — the differentiator from pure-AI shops
- **"shot list"** / **"grade"** / **"cut"** — film vocabulary used
  naturally
- **"AI-native"** — when distinguishing from AI-as-bolted-on-tool
- **"production"** — over "content creation"

## Words and phrases Top Studios NEVER uses

- **"AI-powered"** — generic, applies to every modern tool
- **"creative agency"** — wrong category
- **"branding company"** — wrong category
- **"content creator"** — too generic; describes a job, not a studio
- **"viral"** as a verb or claimed outcome — sounds desperate
- **"on-trend"** / **"trending"** — wrong frame; Top Studios should
  set trends, not chase them
- **"world-class"** / **"best-in-class"** — empty boasts

## Signatures and sign-offs

- Email sign-off: **"— Top Studios"** for project comms.
- Doc footer: **"© 2026 Top Studios"**
- Closing slide: **the studio mark** — minimal. The work has spoken.

## Voice the audience should hear

**The voice of the studio** — institutional, with editorial
sensibility. Confident in its taste without being preachy about it.
A reader should feel they're being addressed by people who know
exactly what they're doing.
