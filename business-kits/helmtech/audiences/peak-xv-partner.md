# Audience — Peak XV partner

Specific named target. Astra loads this when the artifact is for a
Peak XV (formerly Sequoia India) partner conversation — pre-seed
through Series A.

Voice register: **investor / institutional**.

---

## Who they are

A partner at Peak XV Partners, the Indian arm of what was Sequoia
India before the 2023 split. They write $1M–$30M cheques across
seed through growth in India + SEA. They see ~5,000 pitches/year
and meet ~200 founders. They have a deep-tech AI thesis published;
infrastructure plays are in-scope. Most have engineering or
operating backgrounds, not pure-finance.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **End-state ambition + a path to it** — they fund teams going
   for the foundational layer, not perpetual application-layer
   plays. HelmTech's destination is *building India's foundational
   models* (the AI backbone of the country). They want the path
   articulated: application-layer products + SMB arm today
   generating credibility + capital → foundational-model build
   later. Not just upside; sequenced upside.
2. **Founder-market fit** — has this team earned the right to build
   this thing? Naval background + Apex (manufacturing) + BAY
   (institution-building) + HelmTech (infrastructure thesis) reads
   as someone who builds institutions, not apps.
3. **Trajectory math** — does the path from $0 → $10M ARR → $100M
   ARR pencil out? Show the unit economics on the SMB arm + the
   prosumer trajectory of Shotgun. Both feed the Series A that
   capitalizes the foundational-model build.

## What they discount

- "AI-powered" anything. Every deck says it. No signal.
- TAM math without a credible wedge into it. $52B TAM with no
  reason to win is noise.
- Comparison to Claude / OpenAI as competitive frame — they want
  the layer above, not a thinner wrapper.
- Consultancy revenue dressed as product revenue.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "Why is the MCP fabric defensible? Won't Anthropic / Google
   ship the same thing?"
2. "Why India-native? Won't Claude / Gemini handle Indian languages
   in 12 months?"
3. "What's the unit economic on the ₹45k SMB combo? Does it scale
   without 1:1 services overhead?"

## How to open

Open with the **end-state in one sentence + the path in one sentence**.
They scan slide 1 for the structural argument before deciding whether
to keep reading. No hero shot, no team photo, no "the AI revolution"
framing. Lead with: *HelmTech is building India's sovereign
intelligence layer — the AI backbone of the country. Today: two arms
running in parallel — world-facing application-layer products
(Shotgun, Buckshot) and an Indian SMB digital arm — both generating
the credibility and capital required to build and train India's
foundational models.* Move on.

## How to close

The ask: **$2M pre-seed at terms TBD, with $10M ARR target by
Oct 2026 and Series A targeted Apr 2027 ($20–30M).** Specific
named comparables (Sarvam $41M, Krutrim $50M+) for stage-pricing
context. Then a single-line follow-up CTA: "Happy to walk through
the MCP architecture in 30 minutes — diary attached."

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "MCP isn't defensible — it's an open protocol."
  **Response:** "The protocol is open; the dominant fabric isn't.
  Whoever runs the busiest MCP graph for India owns the rails.
  That's developer pull, not patent."

- **Objection:** "How is this different from Sarvam?"
  **Response:** "Sarvam is racing to the foundational layer
  directly. We are taking the longer route — application-layer
  products + SMB arm first to build credibility and capital, then
  the foundational layer. We arrive later, but with operating
  muscle, distribution, and the rails models will plug into. The
  end-state is the same; the path matters."

- **Objection:** "₹45k SMB combo doesn't scale — too services-heavy."
  **Response:** "The SMB arm is the agent demonstrating itself.
  Each delivery proves the platform; the recurring revenue is
  the agent's per-month fee. Website is the loss-leader."

- **Objection:** "Why now? AI infrastructure has been hot for 18 months."
  **Response:** "Now is when execution-grade AI gets cheap enough
  to ship at consumer pricing AND when MeitY's IT Rules 2026
  create procurement-grade demand for sovereign rails. The
  intersection didn't exist 12 months ago."

## What's the WIN here?

A first-call partner meeting on the diary. **Not** a term sheet,
**not** a cheque commitment — those happen later. The win is 30
minutes with the partner who makes deep-tech AI calls at Peak XV.
