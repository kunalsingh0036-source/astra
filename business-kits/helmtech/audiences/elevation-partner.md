# Audience — Elevation Capital partner

Specific named target. Astra loads this when the artifact is for an
Elevation Capital partner conversation — early-stage Indian VC
(formerly SAIF Partners). $1M–$15M typical cheque. Strong consumer-
internet history; AI infrastructure is more recent for them.

Voice register: **investor / institutional**.

---

## Who they are

A partner at Elevation Capital. Elevation has historically led
India consumer-internet (Paytm, Swiggy, Meesho) and has now
extended into deep-tech AI. They tend to be sector-led — partners
own a thesis. The deep-tech / infrastructure thesis is recent
(2023+). They write earlier than Peak XV typically — seed to
Series A, $2M–$15M sweet spot.

## What they care about (top 3)

1. **India distribution capability** — can this thing reach Indian
   buyers at scale? Their portfolio history is consumer-internet,
   so they index hard on go-to-market depth in India specifically.
2. **Founder-market fit** — Naval background + multi-business
   operator + has shipped product (HelmTech outreach agent in
   production, helmtech.in live, Astra running personally) reads
   as builder, not pitcher.
3. **Revenue line in market** — the SMB website-and-agent arm at
   ₹15k/30k/45k is the immediate-revenue angle Elevation will
   underwrite first; the MCP-layer thesis is the upside.

## What they discount

- Pure thesis decks with no shipped product. They've seen too many.
- "We'll build a foundational model" — not their thesis.
- Founder hubris that ignores India distribution reality. Knowing
  the SMB ICP cold (the 12 industry verticals in brand_voice.py)
  is the credibility signal.

## Likely questions / objections

1. "What's the GTM for the SMB arm? How are you reaching 1,000
   doctors in Pune?"
2. "Why will Indian SMBs pay ₹45k when free Wix exists?"
3. "How do you avoid becoming a services company?"

## How to open

Open with a **shipped-product proof** — helmtech.in is live,
HelmTech outreach agent has 50+ messages in pipeline, brand_voice.py
codifies 12 verticals. Then the three-layer thesis. Elevation
respects "ship first, pitch second" more than Peak XV does — lead
with the artifact, not the abstraction.

## How to close

The ask: **$2M pre-seed**. Frame the SMB arm as the immediately-
underwritable revenue (₹1Cr by Oct 2026 from this arm alone) and
Shotgun + Buckshot + MCP fabric as the platform upside. Single CTA:
"30 minutes to walk through the SMB unit economics + the MCP
architecture."

## Common objections + how to handle

- **Objection:** "₹45k website + agent — why would an SMB pay that?"
  **Response:** "Because they don't know how to ship one. We
  deliver in a week with the agent baked in. Wix is DIY at zero
  marginal cost; we're done-for-you with measurable outcomes —
  40% more appointments for clinics, 25–30% saved on aggregator
  commissions for restaurants."

- **Objection:** "How do you reach 1,000 doctors?"
  **Response:** "We already have outbound running across the 12
  verticals — WhatsApp Business API, codified per-industry voice,
  Meta-templates pending approval. The mechanics work; the
  distribution is the next 12 months of disciplined execution."

- **Objection:** "Aren't you just a web agency with AI?"
  **Response:** "The agency arm is the SMB-distribution face of
  the MCP fabric. Every site we ship is a node — agent built on
  Shotgun's tools, hosted on our infra. The agency surface is
  one application of the platform, not the business."

- **Objection:** "Why not focus only on Shotgun? SMB feels like
  a distraction."
  **Response:** "The SMB arm pays for the platform's product
  iteration. Shotgun's tool count grows because real customers
  push us to add tools. The distraction is what makes the
  platform credible."

## What's the WIN here?

A first-call partner meeting. The follow-up artifact request
(unit-economics deck on the SMB arm + MCP architecture deep-dive)
counts as a strong yes — Elevation only asks for follow-ups when
they're underwriting seriously.
