# HelmTech thesis

The narrative IP that doesn't change. Astra reads this as the source
of truth when generating HelmTech artifacts. Every deck, doc, and
email maps back to one of the three layers below.

---

## The thesis in one sentence

HelmTech is India's Intelligence Layer — the foundational models,
MCP protocol fabric, and execution products that let AI *do* things,
not just talk.

## The three layers

1. **Foundational models layer** — sovereign AI capacity. India needs
   its own LLMs trained on Indian data, with Indian governance, in
   Indian languages. Sarvam, Krutrim, BharatGen are early entrants
   here; HelmTech's bet is that the foundational models are the
   commodity layer eventually, not the moat.

2. **MCP layer** — the standardized protocol fabric that lets models
   *act*. Tool-use, agent orchestration, cross-system execution.
   This is the wedge — the lasting differentiation. Every model
   eventually plugs into MCP; whoever builds the dominant MCP fabric
   for India owns the rails.

3. **Execution products layer** — end-user AI products that
   demonstrate what the rails enable. Shotgun (the consumer/prosumer
   AI platform) and Buckshot (AI film-making) are HelmTech's flagship
   products at this layer. Astra (Kunal's personal agent OS) is
   effectively Shotgun v0 — the productized version of what runs
   Kunal himself.

## The wedge

Three things together — none in isolation:

1. **India-native** — built for Indian data, languages, regulation,
   and use cases. Not a port of Anthropic / OpenAI for India.
2. **Execution-first** — AI that acts in the real world (sends emails,
   files compliance, books travel, runs ads). Not a chat toy.
3. **MCP-standardized** — open protocol fabric. Models, tools, and
   agents from anywhere can plug in. The opposite of a walled garden.

## Why now

- India's AI market is consolidating around foundational models;
  the *application* layer is the next wave and is wide open.
- MeitY's IT Rules 2026 (effective Feb 20) impose AI labeling
  requirements — favors platforms with clear provenance and
  governance built in. HelmTech has compliance as a default, not
  retrofit.
- The IndiaAI Mission has explicit funding for deep-tech AI
  startups. NVIDIA + Peak XV + Elevation + Z47 are co-investing
  in this category right now.
- LLM costs have collapsed enough that consumer-priced AI products
  are economically viable. Shotgun's $20–50/month tier is in the
  sweet spot.

## Why not the alternatives

- **vs Sarvam / Krutrim / BharatGen**: They're building the
  commodity layer (foundational models). HelmTech is building the
  layer above — the rails models plug into.
- **vs Claude / ChatGPT / Perplexity**: Foreign-built; subject to
  US data jurisdiction; not India-native. Indian data on Indian
  rails is a real procurement lever for enterprise/government.
- **vs custom enterprise solutions** (TCS, Infosys, Wipro AI arms):
  Slow, services-heavy, project-not-product. HelmTech is product
  velocity at consumer pricing.

## What we're not

To keep the thesis sharp, what HelmTech is *not*:

- Not a foundational model lab (we use them, we don't train them
  from scratch — though we may fine-tune)
- Not an AI consultancy
- Not "ChatGPT for India" — that framing concedes the model is the
  product. The MCP fabric is the product.
- Not a vertical SaaS (Shotgun is horizontal — agents for any work)
